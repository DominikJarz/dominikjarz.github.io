Getting around Poland!


You might have different needs when travelling around Poland - either within a city, accross the country, or comfortably door-to-door using a taxi. Those three apps got you covered!

Poland's public transport systems and culture might differ from your country, therefore in order to avoid unpleasant situations or fines, play a bit with those apps and understand the system here.

This trio of apps is perfect - it will allow you to do any sort of travel in Poland.

Jak dojadę

Jak dojadę means "how will I get there?". This apps allows you to search for the best routes usign public transport wihtin a city. Enter your destination, and it will show you how to get there. You can also buy tickets in specific cities directly in the app, otherwise it will show you where and which ticket to get. A must have not only for foreigners, but for locals wondering when the bus will depart. Remember, in Poland no one will ask you for a ticket to enter a vehicle of city's public transport - it is your responsibility to have it with you, otherwise, if control comes, you might be punished up to 250€!

iOS Android
BILKOM Bilety

BILKOM Bilety (BIL from bilety, meaning tickets, KOM from komunikacyjne, meaning transportation), is an app that allows you to buy mid-range and long-distance train tickets. Do you need to get from Warsaw to Krakow by train? This app will let you look at the timetable of trains and buy the right train ticket.

iOS Android
Bolt

Do you need a taxi? Bolt is the safest way to not get scammed and monitor where your taxi driver is. The service is relatively cheap, especially for foreingers (1.8€ is the minimum price). If you feel like using the scooters on many bike paths available in bigger cities, you can also rent them through the app, for 0.08€ per minute, drive anywhere on the pavement, bike-path and the street and return them anywhere that does not obstruct the traffic flow.
